# Reading List

A semi-exhaustive list of books I hope to read soon

---

# Computer Science & Software Engineering

## Beginner

1. **How to Solve It** — George Pólya  
   *Problem-solving fundamentals; classic but accessible.*

2. **Code: The Hidden Language of Computer Hardware and Software** — Charles Petzold  
   *Excellent introduction to how computers work; highly approachable.*

3. **Grokking Algorithms** — Aditya Bhargava  
   *Visual and beginner-friendly introduction to algorithms.*

4. **The Design of Everyday Things** — Don Norman  
   *Foundational principles of design, usability, and human-centered thinking.*

5. **Head First Design Patterns** — Eric Freeman, Elisabeth Robson, Bert Bates, Kathy Sierra  
   *Accessible introduction to software design patterns.*

---

## Intermediate

6. **Clean Code** — Robert C. Martin  
   *Practical coding practices and maintainable software design.*

7. **The Pragmatic Programmer** — Andrew Hunt, David Thomas  
   *Pragmatic software engineering principles and career-long habits.*

8. **Code Complete** — Steve McConnell  
   *Comprehensive guide to software construction and engineering practices.*

9. **The Mythical Man-Month** — Frederick P. Brooks Jr.  
   *Classic essays on software engineering and project management.*

10. **Clean Architecture** — Robert C. Martin  
    *Architectural principles for building maintainable systems.*

11. **The Principles of Software Design** — Meilir Page-Jones  
    *Core principles behind good software structure and design.*

12. **Computer Networking: A Top-Down Approach** — James F. Kurose, Keith W. Ross  
    *Clear and practical introduction to computer networking.*

13. **The Algorithm Design Manual** — Steven S. Skiena  
    *Practical algorithm design with real-world insight.*

14. **Design Patterns: Elements of Reusable Object-Oriented Software** — Erich Gamma, Richard Helm, Ralph Johnson, John Vlissides  
    *Foundational object-oriented design patterns.*

15. **Refactoring** — Martin Fowler  
    *Improving existing codebases through systematic refactoring.*

---

## Intermediate–Advanced

16. **Designing Data-Intensive Applications** — Martin Kleppmann  
    *Modern distributed systems, databases, scalability, and reliability.*

17. **Domain-Driven Design** — Eric Evans  
    *Strategic and tactical design for complex software systems.*

18. **Implementing Domain-Driven Design** — Vaughn Vernon  
    *Practical implementation of DDD concepts.*

19. **Software Architect's Handbook** — Joseph Ingeno  
    *Software architecture principles, patterns, and responsibilities.*

20. **The Software Engineer's Guidebook** — Gergely Orosz  
    *Industry-oriented engineering practices and career growth.*

21. **The Art of Scalability** — Martin L. Abbott, Michael T. Fisher  
    *Scalable system architecture and operational growth.*

22. **Computer Systems: A Programmer's Perspective** — Randal E. Bryant, David R. O'Hallaron  
    *Bridges programming with underlying computer systems concepts.*

23. **Operating Systems: Three Easy Pieces** — Remzi H. Arpaci-Dusseau, Andrea C. Arpaci-Dusseau  
    *Core operating systems concepts explained clearly.*

24. **Algorithms** — Robert Sedgewick, Kevin Wayne  
    *Rigorous yet practical treatment of algorithms and data structures.*

---

## Advanced

25. **Introduction to Algorithms** — Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L. Rivest, Clifford Stein  
    *Comprehensive and mathematically rigorous algorithms textbook.*

26. **Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs** — Harold Abelson, Gerald Jay Sussman  
    *Deep exploration of abstraction, computation, and programming paradigms.*

27. **Introduction to the Theory of Computation** — Michael Sipser  
    *Theory of computation, automata, and formal languages.*

28. **Compilers: Principles, Techniques, and Tools** — Alfred V. Aho, Monica S. Lam, Ravi Sethi, Jeffrey D. Ullman  
    *Classic compiler construction textbook (“Dragon Book”).*

29. **Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid** — Douglas Hofstadter  
    *Interdisciplinary exploration of logic, consciousness, mathematics, and systems.*

---

## Expert

30. **The Art of Computer Programming** — Donald Knuth  
    *One of the most rigorous and influential works in computer science.*
